# Session 1.7.3 — "Afternight"

*Act 1 — Hammerfall*

## Summary

Dawn after the siege. The council chamber survivors regroup, distribute loot, and absorb the new reality. Balder joins Harald's retinue as Steward. The party then descend back into the Catacombs and complete the first two rooms of the **Temple of Tyr** — the fifth temple of the Pilgrimage.

## Key Events

### Dawn — The Morning After
The Blood Elves withdrew at dawn as all survivors emerge from barricades across Hammerfall. The council chamber holds: Orgrim, Grukk One-Tusk, Harald, Yennen, and the party.

The Speaker is dead. His plans, found on his body, confirm:
- Five nights until Hannah's resurrection ritual at midnight
- The Chosen have a vessel — Ygritte is held in the catacombs

The city does not know what is coming. The party does.

### Loot and State
- **+2 weapon potency rune** — from Ivar's body
- **Resilient rune** — from Ivar's body
- **500gp** — for killing the Speaker
- **Level 3 wand of heal**
- Party advances to **level 7**

**Balder** joins Harald's retinue as Steward, taking on the work of holding the fragmented Norse tribes together. Neither knows they are father and son.

### Temple of Tyr — Entry (Room 1)
The party descend into the Catacombs to continue the Pilgrimage. The Temple of Tyr entrance is a seamless stone wall with a set of scales embedded in the stonework and a circular hole beneath. The inscription reads:

> *Not all blood weighs true. Not all hands should judge. Only the first children may tip the balance and see justice through.*

Gunnar offers his **half-elven blood** — bleeding into the hole. The door opens.

**Loki** makes contact the moment Gunnar bleeds. Amused and wry, he whispers at Gunnar's ear:

> *"Funny thing, isn't it? A door that measures worth... in blood… and here you stand."*
> *"Go on then. Bleed for them. Let's see which half of you the stone remembers."*

The scale in the wall briefly appeared cracked in half as Loki spoke.

### Temple of Tyr — Room 2: Hall of Measured Wrongs
A long hall of polished white stone. Five carved reliefs line the walls, each depicting an elven law and its punishment:
1. "The Mixed Bed" — interracial coupling; exile and shame
2. "The Children's Game" — fraternising with lesser races; separation and correction
3. "The Open Hand" — generosity to outsiders; stripped of equipment
4. "The Broken Boundary" — orc trespassing in elven land; death by roots
5. "The Faithless Hand" — trading with humans for profit; branded; trade partners killed

One relief animated and the party were each assigned a role (Accuser, Defender, Witness, Judge) to preside over a case.

**The party upheld the elven law.** They enforced the harsh punishments of the reliefs and showed themselves in alignment with the elven justice system, despite its cruelty toward other races and its contempt for its own people who associated with them.

The specters nodded. The room remained cold. The exit opened.

## Lore Revealed to Players
- Temple of Tyr requires elven blood — or at least blood of elven lineage — to open
- Loki is aware of Gunnar's mixed heritage and finds it entertaining
- The elven justice system enshrined in the Temple was brutal and supremacist — these were *laws*, not aberrations

## DM Notes
> Private.

The party upholding elven law in the Hall of Measured Wrongs is a notable character moment. These are brutal, discriminatory laws — punishing poverty (the starving orc), punishing kindness (the generous hunter), punishing children for playing together. The party chose to enforce them. This could be:
- A purely mechanical choice (get through the room faster)
- A meaningful character alignment decision with future consequences
- A seed for conflict when Veylith or Caerdan finds out
- Relevant if the party ever faces elven cultural/political decisions
