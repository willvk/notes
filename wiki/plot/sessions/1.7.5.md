# Session 1.7.5 — "Sacrifice"

*Act 1 — Hammerfall*

## Summary

The party finishes the Judgment Construct in Tyr's temple, claims the fifth rune through acts of willing sacrifice, and descends to Depth 4 for the first time. The session ends on a cliffhanger: the Sakapatate fills the far passage and stands waiting — combat begins in 1.8.1.

---

## Key Events

### The Last Verdict — Fenrir Golem Defeated

Combat carries over from 1.7.4. The Judgment Construct reaches 0 HP — its chains pull it back to the platform centre, body resetting, glow dimming to a low steady watch. The Shifting Law mechanic ceases. The arena settles.

With the fight over, the party examines the five reliefs for the first time:

**Ruin side — failed sacrifices:**
- *The Merchant* — offers a coin that costs him nothing; the jaw closes and returns it
- *The Spare Sword* — places a second blade while keeping the first; the plate goes unclaimed

**Order side — true sacrifice rewarded:**
- *The Oath* — sworn words made visible; the wolf's eyes brighten
- *The Warrior's Hand* — placed willingly into the jaw; returned as living chain
- *The Last Weapon* — the only weapon given, leaving the figure unarmed; Tyr's eyes brightest of all

Wall inscriptions scattered between the reliefs: *"Justice without cost is merely preference."* / *"Tyr does not weigh the gift. He weighs the giving."* / *"An oath sworn truly is a chain that binds and armours both."*

The golem's jaw unlocks. Within: the Rune of Tyr on a stone pedestal, old corroded offerings around its base. When lifted, a plate rises beneath — and below where the rune rested, carved in the same precise Elvish:

> *"What do you give willingly, that cannot be returned?"*

### The Sacrifices

**Brawn** placed his hand willingly into the jaw.

The jaw closed — clean, precise — and opened. Where the hand was: living chain-link, pale silver, warm to the touch, fully functional.

**Chain Limb** *(Brawn only):*
Acts as an unarmed Strike (1d8 bludgeoning, **Grapple** trait — can Grapple without a free hand). Once per day (recharges at daily preparations): after a successful unarmed Strike or Grapple with the chain limb, the target must succeed at a **DC [Brawn's class DC] Fortitude save** or become **Restrained** until the end of your next turn.

*Note: DC is Brawn's class DC, intentionally scaling with level — not fixed. See DM Notes.*

---

**Riven** placed Winter's Vigil — the bow given to him by Etta — into the jaw.

The bow was drawn down. The plate sank fully with a resonant click. The golem's eyes brightened. Tyr returned a bow: hardwood, exquisitely made, runes covering its full length.

**Riven's Bow** *(currently unidentified — presenting as a Godsbreath shortbow):*
True nature: a Tyr artifact — see DM Notes for design direction.

---

**Both characters** received a grey scale mark on the back of the wrist — Tyr's acknowledgement. Smooth, warm, permanent.

**Rune of Tyr collected — 5/8.**

---

### Going Down — Depth 4 Entry

The party descends past the Depth 3 landing. The door there is sealed from below — old stonework, five hundred years without movement; whoever sealed it never came back. The passage continues.

The architecture changes immediately below the landing. Confident elven work gives way to shorter, uncertain cuts — half-finished faces, tool marks still exposed in raw limestone. Some sections give way to cave entirely. Old water. Iron. Something that has not seen torchlight in a long time.

Three corridors branch from the entry hall. No markings. No map.

---

### First Signs — Depth 4 Exploration

The party triggered the **pressure plate in R3** (north corridor) — stone spike release, kobold-built crude metalwork. Made considerable noise. A patrol would investigate.

In the wide cavern (R5), **wall markings** were noticed: circuit diagrams in a small precise hand. Routing indicators, danger markers. One symbol repeats on every surface without any check: a sealed door with something pressing hard against it from the other side.

- DC 20 Crafting: navigation diagrams, not decoration
- DC 23 Crafting: partial routing — which passages are regularly used vs. avoided

---

### Cliffhanger — The Sakapatate

Moving deeper into the wide cavern. A shape fills the far passage: stone plates, repurposed metal fittings, the grinding sound of something large and deliberately maintained. It stands in the passage. It is not attacking. It is not retreating.

*Session ends here — 1.8.1 opens mid-combat.*

- Something mechanical and intelligent is living below — traps are maintained and deliberate; the recurring wall symbol suggests a persistent threat being contained on the far side of a sealed door

## DM Notes
> Private.

**Brawn's Chain Limb DC:** Intentionally set to Brawn's class DC rather than fixed at DC 23. This scales with his level and allows future feats/rewards that target his class DC to improve the ability. Do not normalise to a fixed number.

**Riven's Bow — Tyr Artifact Design:**
Currently presenting as a Godsbreath shortbow (unidentified). True nature: a Tyr artifact — a weapon born from the return of Etta's bow through Tyr's jaw. Design direction:

Thematic anchors: judgment, binding, oaths, the irreversible verdict. Tyr gave his hand to bind Fenrir — the bow should carry that weight. Consider:
- Each arrow as a verdict — perhaps they cannot miss a target Riven has sworn to bring to justice
- A binding effect on hit — targets marked by an arrow are impeded in some way (slowed, hindered from retreating)
- Oath-activation — certain features only unlock when Riven has sworn something in Tyr's name
- Potential name: *Verdensdom*, *Oathflight*, *The Binding Arrow* — something that names the verdict-nature

Reveal pacing: the next feature should come naturally from a moment of need or a character choice, not from an Identify spell. Tyr's gifts reveal themselves through use.

**Pressure Plate triggered in R3:** The Sakapatate is in position because the kobolds were alerted. Its presence at the passage entrance in 1.8.1 is deliberate — this was a response, not a coincidence. Use this if the party wonders why it was already there.

**Tyr's Binding** (DC 23 Will → Frightened 2 on successful grapple) — from the prep as the significant sacrifice tier. No PC took this path. Remains unused.

**Countdown:** 1.7.3 began the 5-night countdown. Sessions 1.7.3 through 1.7.5 have taken place in continuous underground time with no long rest. The counter should not have ticked down unless a rest was taken. Confirm with party before 1.8.1.
