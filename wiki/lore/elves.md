# Elves
*The first children of the gods — ancient, diminishing, and secretive*

## Overview
The Elves were the first children of the gods. Given many gifts, they raised great cities, conquered the wild lands of Valkair through harnessed magical power and great walls. An experiment between the immortality of the gods and the frailty of mortals.

For reasons unknown, the Elves have been dwindling. Perhaps the prevalence of the "barbaric races" and interbreeding, or perhaps they are responsible for their own downfall.

## Society
A strict class system and selective monarchy — the High King or Queen is selected from noble families. Due to long lifespans, a new ruler is rarely needed.

**Five noble houses (recorded):**
- [[Thrandurassil]] — ruled [[Dres Elas]] and the known world
- [[Netherese]]
- [[Finarfin]]
- [[Earendil]]
- [[Idril]]

## Cultural Law: Blood Purity
The Elves suffer no dilution of the elven bloodline. Mixing elven blood with any other species is treated as blasphemy against the gods — one of the highest crimes in Elven society. Any mixed-blood child is cast out and forfeits all birthrights.

This is the social law that produced [[Hannah]] — cast out as a shame to House Thrandurassil, hidden among farmers.

## Relationship to Other Races
Elves only tolerate the "barbaric races" temporarily in times of trade. They are mysterious, advanced, and withholding — hoarding their secrets and looking down on all others.

Lately elven cities have become ruins, the ruling class missing. Elven nobility is now even more reclusive, and their secrets are retreating beyond reach.

## Role in Valkair
Elves wield true arcane mastery in a low-magic world — the only race that does. This makes them simultaneously invaluable and terrifying. Their cities are the architecture of the world; their absence leaves ruins.

The Elves' long-term goal (campaign-wide): **to kill the gods.** Act 3 is described as "Oedipus Elves — Hearts & Minds."
