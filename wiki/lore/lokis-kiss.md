# Loki's Kiss
*A cursed ring bearing Loki's influence — currently worn by Gunnar*

## Overview
A ring discovered by [[Brelka]] during her time as a Finger in [[Loki's Hand]]. Loki's influence flowing through it drove Brelka to betray her faction and her friends — the dark irony being that Loki used his own Hand against itself.

Brelka was killed by the party in session 1.2.8. [[Gunnar]] took the ring and is now wearing it.

## Known Effects (on Gunnar)
- **Loki's presence** — Loki has been aware of and watching Gunnar since Midsommerblót (session 1.4.01), where he appeared as a cheeky observer in Gunnar's visions rather than an active force
- **Direct contact** — Loki has spoken with Gunnar twice: once at the entrance to the Temple of Tyr, and once during the hunt for [[lore/hrimgaldr|Hrimgaldr]], when Loki provided information on how to find the Gods' Glade where the beast was located
- **Rat transformation** — grants a small ability to transform into a rat at will

## Known Effects (on Brelka)
- Drove paranoia, distrust, and an escalating compulsion toward betrayal and violence
- Combined with the wererat lycanthropy, accelerated her descent

## Lore Significance
The ring is a channel for Loki's direct influence. This is not merely a magic item — it is Loki taking an interest. The visions Gunnar receives are Loki communicating (or testing, or playing).

The rat transformation echoes Brelka's lycanthropy — possibly Loki's thematic signature on the ring, or a residual effect from Brelka's time wearing it.

## Story Potential — The Bargain
Loki's next move is likely an offer: improved transformation ability through Loki's Kiss, potentially including combat forms. This is an escalation of what the ring already grants.

The cost will be something Loki enjoys — not gold or service, but acts that fit his nature:

| Cost type | Examples |
|-----------|---------|
| **Betrayal** | Deceive or betray a party member, ally, or faction in a way that serves Loki's entertainment |
| **Trickery** | Execute a deception with real stakes — not petty lies, something that reshapes a relationship |
| **Character-shaking consequence** | Something that forces Gunnar to act against his own values or identity |

The tone should be: the offer is almost reasonable, the cost is deeply uncomfortable, and the full consequence isn't clear until it lands. Loki isn't lying — he just enjoys watching the ripples.

Gunnar's supernatural mirror (from his burial tomb backstory) and the ring are two separate divine threads on one character. If both become active simultaneously this could create genuine internal conflict.

## Contradictions & Open Questions
- ❓ Is the rat transformation a feature of the ring itself or a remnant of Brelka's lycanthropy?
- ❓ When Loki made contact at the Temple of Tyr and during the Hrimgaldr hunt — what was his tone and manner? Helpful but unsettling? Openly amused? This shapes how future contact feels consistent.
- ❓ Has Gunnar told the rest of the party about Loki's contacts, or is he keeping it to himself?
