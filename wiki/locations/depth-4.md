# Depth 4 — The Abandoned Level

*The lowest accessible section of the Shallows catacombs. The elves started building here and abandoned it when Hannah became the Forsaken (~500 years ago). The kobold tribe has lived here ever since, finishing what they could, sealing what they feared, and building their own civilisation in the ruins.*

---

## Overview

Depth 4 is a mix of:
- **Partially constructed elven stonework** — grand arched ceilings in some sections, half-cut walls in others, tool marks still visible
- **Kobold additions** — cruder, improvised, practical; traps, scaffolding, forges, housing
- **Natural cave** — the deeper and more peripheral sections give way to raw limestone entirely

The party arrives with no map. There are no labels. The temples are not obviously identifiable — they are ruins, repurposed, or half-built. The party may need to backtrack and realise "oh, that was Odin's temple" after the fact.

**Runes still needed:** Odin (R10), Thor (R15), Freya (sealed — R19, accessed via Hollow Coin)

## Room Keys

---

### R1 — Entry Hall
**Type:** Junction. No battle map needed.

The base of the central staircase. A rough-hewn antechamber where elven construction begins and immediately becomes unfinished. Three passages branch: west (R2), north (R3, main route), east (R4). The ceiling is high — started as a grand vaulted hall, never completed. One wall has three corbels carved but no arch above them. Torches the party carries are the first fire here in centuries.

---

### R2 — West Dead End
**Type:** Exploration, no encounter.

A short corridor ending in a cave-in. The stonework gives way abruptly to raw rock — the elves stopped here. Some carved decorative work on the surviving wall: a faint wave pattern (Njord motif — left over from an earlier survey route). Old, dry, nothing of value. Dead end.

*Purpose: waste a little time, reward thoroughness with a decorative detail.*

---

### R3 — North Corridor (Pressure Plate)
**Type:** Trap corridor.

A long straight corridor of half-finished elven stonework, the walls showing rough tool marks. Midway:

**Trap — Pressure Plate** (DC 20 Perception to notice)
Stone spike release, still functional. Not elven — kobold-built, crude metalwork, installed within the last century. The mechanism is as much alarm as weapon: it makes noise when triggered. If triggered, assume a kobold patrol investigates within 5 minutes.

- Disable: DC 20 Thievery
- Damage on trigger: 2d8+5 piercing, DC 23 Reflex save for half

---

### R4 — East Alcove (Workstation)
**Type:** Exploration, no encounter.

A natural cave pocket off the entry hall, partially walled in with rough kobold stonework. A low stone bench covered in small tools: gears, pins, coils of wire, half-finished carvings. Everything scaled for a knee-height builder. The tools are recently used — warm metal. A half-completed device sits on the bench (purpose unclear — could be a trap mechanism, could be part of a larger construct).

*Players can make DC 20 Crafting to understand the device is a component for a larger mechanical assembly — consistent with the Sakapatate construct they'll see later.*

---

### R5 — Wide Cavern
**Type:** Exploration / first sighting. No battle map needed but could use one.

A wide natural cavern breaking the elven construction. Natural stone columns rising from the floor, stalactites overhead. The first space that feels genuinely ancient and non-elven.

This is where the party first sees the **Sakapatate** — it moves between the columns, heavy and deliberate. Grinding stone, clicking mechanisms. It does not approach. It watches, then withdraws deliberately — making sure it was seen.

**Kobold wall markings appear here for the first time.** Circuit diagrams on every flat surface:
- DC 20 Crafting: navigation diagrams, not decoration — deliberate routing maps
- DC 23 Crafting: extract partial routing — can tell which passages are marked "frequently used" vs. "avoid"
- No check needed: one symbol repeats everywhere — a sealed door with something pressing hard against it from the other side

*Battle map prompt (for Grok):*
> "Dungeon battle map, top-down view, 1 inch = 5 feet grid, 30x30 feet. A wide natural cavern with uneven stone floor, four large natural stone columns rising from the floor, stalactites hanging from ceiling. Torchlight from the left side. One passage in, two passages out. Dark fantasy aesthetic, no people, no monsters. Stone walls with some crude carved diagrams/circuit patterns. Some kobold-scale scaffolding in one corner."

---

### R6 — The Narrows (Wire Tripline)
**Type:** Trap corridor.

A narrow passage, natural cave walls pressing close. Shin-height wire across the path.

**Trap — Wire Tripline** (DC 18 Perception to notice)
Releases a rack of hanging stone weights from above. Loud. Functions primarily as an alarm — the noise will bring a kobold patrol within 3 minutes.

- Disable: DC 18 Thievery
- Damage on trigger: 2d6 bludgeoning to anyone in the 10-foot section, DC 20 Reflex save for half. Much more importantly: loud.

---

### R7 — Outer Ward
**Type:** Key junction. Potential encounter.

A wider constructed space — the elves built this as a proper antechamber to something grander, but never finished it. Doorways are arched but doorless. Two exits: northwest toward R8 (Red Corridor), east toward R9 (Settlement Gate).

**Kobold patrol** — 2 guards with a small mechanical device (not a Sakapatate — a simpler single-pilot scout unit, essentially a kobold in a stone shell with spike arms). Not aggressive unless the party moves toward R8. If approached friendly: they gesture firmly away from R8 and toward R9.

*This is the first place the party understands they're in kobold territory, not just kobold proximity.*

---

### R8 — Red Corridor Approach (SEALED)
**Type:** Major encounter if forced. Primarily a barrier and horror reveal.

A corridor of heavy elven construction — this section was actually completed, the most finished stonework on this level. Grand arched ceiling, carved drainage channels running floor-to-midwall along both sides.

The channels were built for water.

They are stained dark — rust-brown from floor to mid-wall, the colour of blood soaked centuries deep into porous stone. Not fresh. But not entirely dry either. Somewhere ahead, distantly, the sound of dripping.

**The Sakapatate (primary) is positioned here.** Three kobold pilots inside, active and alert. This is their best machine and their last line on this side of the seal.

Behind it, further down the corridor: a massive stone barrier the kobolds built themselves, two centuries ago. Sealed with their own stonework and their own engineering. On the barrier, carved repeatedly in Draconic:

> *"The Red Below. Do Not Open. Do Not Open. Do Not Open."*

If the party tries to pass, Vress's representative arrives quickly:
> *"We do not negotiate on this. Three of ours taken in the last moon. If you wake what lives in there, you will not survive it. Neither will we."*

**If somehow forced past the seal:** The blood channels run fresh here. The smell is overwhelming. Somewhere deep in the passage, something moves. This is not a winnable fight at level 7 — this is the Chosen's operational base beneath Upper Hammerfall. A glimpse only, then retreat.

*Battle map prompt (for Grok):*
> "Dungeon battle map, top-down view, 1 inch = 5 feet grid, 20x40 feet. A wide fortified corridor with grand elven arched ceiling (implied by walls). Deep rust-brown staining along the walls at mid-height from ancient blood. Two carved drainage channels running along both sides of the floor. At one end, a crude heavy stone barrier wall with Draconic script carved repeatedly. A large stone construct (mechanical spider-like creature with multiple piloted seats visible) positioned in front of the barrier. Torchlight only, dark and oppressive atmosphere. No people visible."

---

### R9 — Settlement Gate
**Type:** Transition. No battle map needed.

A carved elven archway — one of the few completed decorative elements on this level. The arch is intact but the door is gone. Through it, the kobold settlement begins. Rope and wire indicators hang from the arch — some kind of signalling system (tugging certain wires alerts the settlement).

---

### R10 — Matriarch's Hall (Kobold Settlement)
**Type:** Social encounter, major location. Battle map recommended.

A repurposed unfinished elven grand chamber — the largest space on this level. Vaulted ceiling 30 feet high, ambitious scale, abandoned mid-build. The kobolds have filled it over five centuries.

**Features:**
- Stone platforms at varying heights (4–12 feet), connected by rope bridges and ladders
- Three small forges along the east wall, constantly lit
- Gear assemblies and machines in various states of completion
- A communal floor space at centre — where the party is brought
- The **primary Sakapatate** sits dormant at the north end, pilot seats empty. It is enormous. When active, it fills the space between platforms.

60–80 kobolds visible. They stop what they're doing when the party enters.

**Vress** sits elevated on a modified elven throne-base at the north end. **Skit** at her shoulder.

*Battle map prompt (for Grok):*
> "Dungeon battle map, top-down view, 1 inch = 5 feet grid, 50x50 feet. A grand but unfinished vaulted stone chamber, high arched ceiling implied. Multiple stone platforms at different heights connected by rope bridges and rope ladders. Three forges against one wall with glowing coals. Many small workbenches covered in mechanical parts. At the far end, a massive stone construct the size of a large carriage, dormant, with visible mechanical joints and multiple seat-like recesses. Torchlit, warm light. No people — just the environment."

---

### R11 — Storage (East)
**Type:** Exploration.

Kobold storage — crates, salvaged elven stonework, tools. Functional and uninteresting. However: among the stored items, Vress keeps a small locked box containing objects recovered from near R17 (the sealed Freya entrance) over the centuries. If the party has Vress's trust, she may show these:

- A carved ivory comb, still smooth despite age, depicting interlocking flowers
- A small glass vial, still sealed, containing a liquid that smells faintly of something sweet and unfamiliar
- Three coins of a design no one recognises (pre-conquest Dres Elas currency, with Freya's symbol)

*These are the Hollow Coin thread items. Don't introduce them unless trust is established.*

---

### R12 — Forge Room
**Type:** Exploration, optional RP.

The main kobold forge. Two kobold smiths working on construct components. They will not stop working and will not engage unless addressed directly. One of them is modifying what appears to be an arm assembly for the large Sakapatate.

If asked about the sealed shrine (R17), the older smith — without stopping work — says through Skit:
> *"We have checked every joint of the door for three hundred years. It has no joint. It is not a door. It is just wall. But it sounds hollow."*

---

### R13 — Workshop (West)
**Type:** Exploration.

Smaller workshop. Trap mechanisms in various states of assembly. A kobold engineer here is the one who built R3 and R6 — she will notice if the party triggered either trap (she can tell from the reset mechanism condition) and will be quietly judgmental about it.

---

### R14 — Deep Passage
**Type:** Corridor junction, transition to temple zone.

Beyond the settlement, the stonework becomes more unfinished — half-arched ceilings, rough floors. Natural cave begins to intrude. The passage forks: northwest to R15 (Odin Temple), east to R18 (Thor Temple). A dead-end branch south leads to R17 (Freya sealed entrance).

The kobolds rarely come this far. Their markings thin out. It feels older and stranger.

---

### R15 — Odin Temple (Plain Room)
**Type:** Puzzle encounter. Battle map recommended.

**What it looks like:** A plain, rectangular room. Low ceiling compared to the grand chambers above. The kobolds have been using it as secondary storage — crates against the walls, some broken furniture salvaged from elsewhere. Nothing obviously holy or significant.

**What it actually is:** The entrance to Odin's hall, sealed beneath the floor.

**Features:**
- Two stone perches mounted on opposite walls — empty. Sized for large birds. Players may not notice their significance at first.
- A single rope hanging from the ceiling, fraying at the bottom. Old. The knot at the top looks deliberate — tied to something, not a safety rope.
- An altar at the far end, barely recognisable under kobold storage. A bowl-shaped depression in the top, centre. Dry. Clean — the kobolds have left it alone instinctively.
- Carved into the walls: raven motifs, repeated. At first glance decorative. On examination they are not random — each raven faces a specific direction and has a symbol carved into its wing.

**The Puzzle — One Eye:**
The mechanism to open the floor is encoded in the raven carvings. With both eyes open the carvings look like wall decoration. With one eye covered — the shadows cast by the carvings form a sequence pointing to a specific order of symbols around the altar bowl.

- A player who covers one eye and studies the carvings: DC 20 Perception or DC 23 Arcana to notice the shadow pattern
- Once noticed, the sequence is readable without further checks
- Touching the altar bowl symbols in the correct order: a deep grinding sound. A section of the floor (roughly 10x10 feet, near the altar) descends slowly, revealing a staircase down to R16

*If a player touches the rope:* it holds weight. If someone hangs from it — they dangle in the centre of the room. From that position, all the raven carvings point directly at them. The sequence is immediately obvious.

**Connecting to Odin:** Odin hung himself from Yggdrasil for nine days. The rope is not a clue — it is the answer. The correct "key" is hanging from it while looking at the ravens with one eye covered. Doing this triggers the floor opening automatically. A player who does this without solving the puzzle first receives the experience of understanding washing over them — Odin's presence for a moment — and the floor opens.

*Battle map prompt (for Grok):*
> "Dungeon battle map, top-down view, 1 inch = 5 feet grid, 25x30 feet. A plain rectangular stone room with low ceiling. Crates and broken furniture pushed to the walls. Two carved stone perches mounted on opposite walls (bird-sized). A single rope hanging from the ceiling at centre. An altar block at the far end with a shallow bowl depression. Walls covered in repeated carved raven motifs, each raven facing a different direction. Dim torchlight. Stone floor with a faint seam forming a 10x10 square near the altar."

---

### R16 — Odin's Hidden Sanctum
**Type:** Reward room / lore encounter.

Accessed from R15 via the descending floor section. A small, immaculate chamber — unlike anything else in Depth 4. The elves completed this one. The walls are covered in runic carvings (not Draconic — something older). At the centre: a single stone plinth with a circular recess.

The Odin Rune sits in the recess. It does not pulse when approached — it waits, still. Taking it requires nothing. No combat. No test. Odin already watched them figure it out.

On the walls, if anyone can read the old runic script (DC 28 Arcana, or automatic if the character has divine connection to Odin):
> *"The cost of knowing is always the same. What you see with both eyes is the world as others show it to you. What you see with one is the world as it is."*

**Rune collected: Odin (6/8)**

---

### R17 — Freya Temple Entrance (SEALED)
**Type:** Dead end / hook.

A dead-end branch off R14. The passage ends in a perfectly smooth stone wall. No door, no seam, no handle. The kobolds have checked every inch of it for centuries — it has no opening mechanism on this side.

The floor in front of it is worn smooth. Old offerings left here — flowers long since dust, coins, small carved figures. Someone has been leaving them for a very long time.

On the wall above the sealed stone, barely visible: a carved flower motif. Freya's symbol.

*This is the Hollow Coin hook. The other side of this wall is the Freya temple interior — accessible only via the teleportation circle at The Hollow Coin in Richeri.*

---

### R18 — Thor Temple
**Type:** Combat/challenge encounter. Battle map recommended.

**What it looks like:** A large chamber, partially flooded — ankle-to-knee deep water covering the floor. The ceiling is high, natural cave blending into elven-started vaulting. Six stone pillars rise from the water, arranged in two rows. The water is cold and still.

**What makes it strange:** The pillars are magnetised. Metal weapons and armour are pulled toward them at close range. Moving between pillars with metal equipment requires effort (DC 17 Athletics to move through difficult terrain created by magnetic pull when within 10 feet of a pillar). The pillars cannot be destroyed — they are deep-anchored stone.

**The encounter — The Storm Warden:**
A bound elemental guardian, dormant in the water. When the party steps more than 20 feet into the chamber it activates. Not undead — something divine and angry, bound here as a test. Storm and lightning aesthetics.

*Full stat block TBD — level 8-9 creature, storm/lightning themed, uses the water and pillars as environmental advantages. The magnetic pillars should create interesting tactical decisions.*

**After defeat:**
The water drains slowly. The floor beneath reveals the Thor Rune carved into the stone at the chamber's centre.

**Rune collected: Thor (7/8)**

*Battle map prompt (for Grok):*
> "Dungeon battle map, top-down view, 1 inch = 5 feet grid, 40x40 feet. A large stone chamber with ankle-deep water covering the entire floor (blue-grey water, reflective). Six large stone pillars rising from the water in two rows of three. High ceiling transitioning from carved stone arches to natural cave. The water is still and dark. No people or monsters. A carved symbol (lightning bolt / hammer) barely visible on the floor beneath the water at the centre. Dark and oppressive, minimal light."

## DM Notes

### Image Generation (Grok/Midjourney)
Each room marked "Yes" for battle map has a prompt in its description. Feed these to Grok or Midjourney. For Foundry VTT import, generate at 140px per grid square (standard Foundry resolution) and set the scale in scene settings.

### Kobold Encounters
The kobolds are not a combat faction unless the party forces it at R8. They function as a social faction with their own agenda. Vress's trust unlocks:
- R11 storage items (Freya/Hollow Coin hook)
- Routing information from the wall markings
- Non-hostile passage through R7 and R9

### Countdown Integration
Each long rest taken in Depth 4 = one night. The nightly attacks are happening above (Upper Hammerfall). The party is insulated from them down here — but each rest makes the clock louder. Track:
- Night 1: 5 nights remaining (end of 1.7.3)
- Each long rest: −1 night
- At 0: midnight ritual, Hannah resurrected

### What Kobolds Know
Vress can confirm:
- Three unfinished shrines (R15, R17, R18) — she doesn't know which god
- R17 is sealed and hollow-sounding
- The Red Below (R8) has gotten worse in the last month
- They have objects from R17's doorstep (R11 storage)
