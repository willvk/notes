The party fights the Sakapatate, makes first contact with the kobold civilisation beneath Hammerfall, and meets Vress and Skit in the settlement.

## Opening Monologue

*Pick up mid-combat. The Sakapatate is in position at the far passage — the party is in the wide cavern. The machine is already waiting.*

---

You came down here for runes. You came down here to stop a resurrection.

You came down here into the dark, into something half-built and abandoned, and you set off every trap in the first corridor.

Now something large is standing in the passage ahead of you.

You do not know what it is. You do not know who built it. You do not know if it will let you through.

*You know what it sounds like when it moves.*

#### The Arm Break

*When the Sakapatate reaches 95 HP, read aloud:*

---

A crack — sharp as a whip — from inside the machine's left shoulder joint. The arm drops. The construct lurches sideways, grinding stone, and a hatch blows open near the elbow.

A kobold tumbles free — rolling badly, landing in a heap.

The exterior pilots go silent.

The machine does not advance.

---

The ejected pilot must succeed at a **DC 20 Acrobatics check** or take **2d6 bludgeoning damage** from the fall.

The Sakapatate **loses Divided Controls** — one Strike per turn only.

*But the fight is over.*

One exterior pilot scrambles down from the shoulder and runs into the dark — not toward the party, away. Going to report. The other two hold position, watching. They are not attacking. The machine broke. The things did not die.

*Give the table the silence. Nothing in the kobolds' experience prepared them for this.*

## Scene 2 — First Contact

### The Scouts
---
*After a few minutes — small movements in the dark. Eyes at the column edges.*

---

Two young kobolds step into the edge of the torchlight. They move slowly, deliberately non-threatening. One holds a small instrument — wire and carved bone — that clicks softly as it sweeps toward the party. It is measuring something. Neither kobold speaks Common. Neither attacks.

They look at each other. The one with the instrument checks its reading and speaks — Draconic, careful, the kind of voice used when reporting observations:

> *"Not-rotten. Not-blood-smell. Not shadow-thing."*
> *(checking the instrument again)*
> *"It came from the upper dark. The sealed place."*

They confer quietly. One disappears back into the dark. The other stays — watching, instrument still raised.

After a few minutes the first returns with a third kobold: older, scarred, carrying a staff of repurposed elven stonework. He speaks with the authority of someone delivering a message, not making a decision:

> *"You are permitted passage. The matriarch will speak with you. Do not go toward the red corridor."*

*He does not explain who told him. Vress already knew before the scout reached her.*

---

## Scene 3 — Vress and Skit

### R10 — The Matriarch's Hall
---
*Led deeper into the level, the party reaches the kobold settlement: a repurposed unfinished elven grand chamber. Vaulted arches in the bones of it, ambitious scale, abandoned mid-build. The kobolds have filled it with platforms on rope bridges, small forges, gear assemblies, machines at various stages of completion.*

*At the centre: a Sakapatate. Dormant, pilot positions empty. It is larger than the one they just fought. Older. Its stone surface is carved with pictorial markings covering the full surface — record-keeping, histories, something between engineering log and scripture. This machine is not primarily a weapon.*

*Sixty, maybe eighty kobolds. They all stop when the party enters.*

---

**Vress** sits elevated on a modified elven throne-base. Old beyond reckoning — grey-scaled, three fingers missing from her left hand, one eye milky and sightless. She does not speak Common or modern Elvish. Only old dialect and Draconic.

**Skit** stands at her shoulder. Young, quick, translates without editorialising — at first.

---

*Vress studies the party for a long moment. Her good eye moves across each of them. She already has a conclusion.*

> *"You came through the sealed door. You came from the halls above."*
>
> *"You fought the stone wolf."*

*Not a question.*

> *"You are the first people of the builders to come from those halls in five hundred years. The last ones came from that direction, sealed the passage at the level above, and never returned. We assumed they died."*
>
> *"We have been completing their work since then. It is wrong to leave things unfinished."*

*Then, carefully:*

> *"Did you make a sacrifice? In the hall above?"*

*She wants to know. It matters to her — not as a test, but as history. She has waited a very long time to ask this question.*

---

#### What Vress Will Share

- They have been here since **the Great Betrayal** — their name for the day Hannah became the Forsaken. *"The day the red began."* They were a small displaced tribe. The elves pulled back and left. What was abandoned became theirs.
- There are **three unfinished shrines** deeper in. The kobolds have been trying to complete them for generations. They know the spaces. They do not know what the shrines are for or what belongs in them.
- One shrine is **sealed**. They cannot open it. They left offerings outside it for a century before giving up. *"It does not want us."*
- The **red corridor** connects to the place of screaming, above and to the east. It has grown worse lately. *"The blood flows warmer now. Three of ours taken in the last moon. Something above has changed."* They sealed it themselves two hundred years ago. Their best machine guards it.

#### What Vress Wants

> *"You came from the builders' halls. You know what those places are for. We have been finishing them for four hundred years without knowing why. What are the shrines? What should be in them?"*

*She has carried this question her entire life. It is not a negotiation. It is genuine.*

## Scene 4 — Options

### What Now
---
*After the conversation with Vress, the party has choices. Don't push — let them drive.*

---

#### The Red Corridor (R8)
If the party approaches the red corridor — a Sakapatate activates immediately. Every kobold in sight stops.

Vress, through Skit:
> *"We do not negotiate on this."*

If they only want to look: let them see. Grand elven construction, arched ceilings, carved drainage channels. The channels were built for water. They are stained dark — rust-brown from floor to mid-wall, the colour of blood soaked centuries deep into porous stone. Distantly, the sound of dripping. Something still flows.

#### Settlement Exploration
- **R12 — Forge:** Two kobold smiths working on construct components. One is modifying an arm assembly for the large Sakapatate. If asked about the sealed shrine: *"We have checked every joint for three hundred years. It has no joint. It is not a door. It is just wall. But it sounds hollow."*
- **R13 — Workshop:** The kobold engineer who built the traps in R3 and R6. She will notice if the party triggered them. She will be quietly judgmental.
- **R11 — Storage:** Vress's locked box of objects recovered from near the sealed shrine (carved ivory comb, sealed glass vial, three unrecognised coins). Only surfaces if the party has established real trust.

#### The Deep Passage (R14)
Beyond the settlement, the kobolds rarely go. The stonework becomes more unfinished — natural cave intruding. The passage forks: northwest toward R15 (Odin Temple), east toward R18 (Thor Temple). A dead-end branch south leads to R17 (Freya — sealed, accessible only from the other side).

*Don't push the party toward the temples this session unless they choose it. The Vress conversation is the heart of 1.8.1.*
